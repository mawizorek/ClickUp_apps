01 · The core split

Two clocks. Keep them apart.

Every past headache came from mixing content that changes yearly with a schedule that reshuffles termly. Split them into separate records; let a join map one onto the other.

Permanent · rebuilt never

Curriculum · Decks · Assets

How you teach a topic, the Freeform board that carries it, the fixture photo inside. Notes accumulate here across every semester. A vault.

03 · The wiring

How the links run.

Every arrow is a ClickUp Relationship field, bidirectional. Set one side, the other shows automatically.

Sessioncovers →Curriculum
Sessionuses →Deck
Deckteaches →Curriculum
Assetappears in →Curriculum
Curriculumwrites out to →Script Doc

One sentence: a Session covers Topics, taught from Decks, which show Assets, while each Topic's prose lives in a Doc. A session with a topic that has no deck = a visible gap. That's your completeness check.

05 · Getting content in

Harvest, don't hand-key.

The point is streamlining, so we mine existing lists instead of retyping. First target: [124] LECTURE Layouts (39 decks), the spine everything hangs on.

  1. Lift the decks.Each top-level Lecture Layouts task → a Deck. Its topic subtasks → Curriculum records, linked via Teaches. De-dupes the two overlapping lists in one move.
  2. Split logistics out."Reserve SPAC," "file paperwork," "submit evals" are NOT curriculum → route to the semester ENGL 124 TASKS list. Curriculum shrinks 76 → ~25-30 topics.
  3. Seed Script Docs.Prose in the ENGL 124 Notes pages links into each topic's Script Doc field. Written content leaves the monolithic doc.
  4. Pull assets.Images embedded in old pages become Asset records, linked to topics. They cross-reference instead of sitting inline.
  5. Stamp one term of Sessions.Only after the vault proves out. Empty dated slots, anchor-tagged, pointed at topics + decks. The clone-forward template for every future term.
06 · Pressure test — "Electrical Theory"

What the real data surfaced.

Ran one topic through the model against the live task. It held, and got sharper for the bruises.

Findings baked into the schema above
  • PART 1 / PART 2 drift, live. Same topic in two lists diverging — exactly what the split kills.
  • 12 loose attachments on one topic. Vindicates the Assets list hard.
  • Blank vs Annotated editions were the miss. Decks now carry both, feeding Blackboard directly.
  • Three sequencing signals fought (Phase, Layout Number, dependencies). Kept one: Seq + Anchor on Sessions, Phase on Curriculum. Killed the rest.
  • Granularity blessed: Intensity + Electrical Theory = two topics sharing one deck. The join's whole point.
