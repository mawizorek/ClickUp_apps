My top three

Chosen for defensible moat, clean burst boundaries, and real competitive whitespace, not just a high score. One build-once asset, one high-ceiling service, one clean production service with a caveat.

01 · GPT · Digital asset

Venue Digital Twin Licensing

Build accurate Vectorworks models of small and midsize venues once, license each file repeatedly to incoming designers. The strongest whitespace on the board: the market is fragmented into free venue giveaways and bespoke one-offs, with no catalog player.

Real competitors
Edgelight RGB · VWX Theater Model$25A generic, customizable theater model. Not a real venue, no verified data. Commodity floor, not a competitor to venue-specific twins.
Vectorworks OutsourcingPer projectGeneral drafting-for-hire (events, scenery, theatre). Sells labor by the job, not a reusable venue library.
Individual designers (Colby Anderson, Dani Clifford, Matt Grenier)PortfolioBuild venue base files as one-off portfolio pieces. Proof the skill is valued, but nobody productizes it.
Venues themselves (e.g. Rothstein Theatre)FreeLarger houses post their own Vectorworks plots free. Your target is the majority of venues that have nothing usable.
The gap: no one runs a verified, maintained catalog of small-venue twins licensed to designers. Generic models are cheap and useless; real ones are free-if-they-exist or costly bespoke. That middle is empty.
02 · Claude · High-value consulting

Second-Brain Workspace Consulting

Package the git-backed agent, memory, and workflow system you already run daily. Highest ceiling, most crowded field. The moat is not "I set up ClickUp," it is a live, versioned agent system you can demo, which almost none of the incumbents can show.

Real competitors
camelai$8,800Verified ClickUp implementation for teams on EOS, plus an AI-automation tier at the same price. The high end of generic done-for-you.
Tuck Consulting Group$750+/moClickUp Ruby Partner, ongoing managed services and training. This is the recurring-retainer model you would compete with.
Robyn Henke$350 – full buildFreelancer-focused: a 90-minute Process Reset at $350 up to a 14-day Full Build. Closest to a solo-operator positioning.
ConsultEvo, BlueCreative, Solution Integrators, stackset, Ellie RogersCustomA deep bench of "verified ClickUp consultant" shops all selling audits, cleanups, automations. The category is mature.
Reality check: this is a red ocean. Generic ClickUp setup is a commodity. You only win by narrowing hard to the git-versioned agent architecture as the demo, a thing these incumbents structurally do not have.
03 · GPT · Analysis service

Script Breakdown Bureau

Turn a script into a production-intelligence packet before departments burn a week rediscovering it. Cleanest burst profile of the three, but it carries the most active competitive threat, so it ranks third on purpose.

Real competitors
ShowBuildSaaSThe one direct theatrical player: automates pre-production breakdown into scene analysis, department breakdowns, and cue suggestions. Watch this one.
AIScriptReader$29 / scriptAI tags 15 production categories per scene, editable. This is the price floor your human judgment has to clear.
Filmustage, Scriptflow, Studiovity, MartinSaaSMature film/TV AI breakdown tools. Not theatre-focused, but they set buyer expectations for speed and cost.
The threat: AI is commoditizing the tagging layer at ~$29 a script. What survives is theatrical production-load judgment (practicals, transitions, staffing pressure, staging risk) the tools can't infer. Sell the interpretation, never the tag list, or a bot underbids you.
